Project Atlas

The primary goal of this mission is to collect samples from an asteroid and return them to Earth for scientific analysis. By studying these samples, we hope to gain insights into the early solar system, planetary formation, and potential resources for future space exploration.

Road Map

Phase 1: Launch

During liftoff of the Falcon Heavy, all engines ignite simultaneously. As the rocket ascends, the cores burn their fuel sequentially. Once a core’s fuel is depleted, it separates from the rocket and returns for reuse. The remaining cores continue the ascent, eventually releasing the payload into orbit.

Phase 2: Orbit and Gravity Assist

After establishing a stable Earth orbit, the Atlas probe will fire its engines to increase its velocity. This boost allows the probe to reach its escape velocity. Before traveling to the asteroid Benu, the probe will intercept Jupiter and use it's massive gravitational pull to increase it's velocity towards Benu.

Phase 3: Interception and Sample Collection

Once the Atlas probe has intercepted the asteroid Benu, it will collect surface samples to analyze the composition of the ancient asteroid. To acomplish this feat, a "Touch-and-Go" method will be executed. The spacecraft orbits the asteroid, scans its surface, and identifies a suitable sampling site. It then descends to the surface, briefly touches down (like a gentle tap), and collects a sample of the asteroid’s material.

Phase 4: Return and Splashdown

The capsule’s trajectory back to Earth is carefully calculated to ensure it enters the atmosphere at the correct angle. Too steep, and it risks burning up; too shallow, and it may skip back into space. Once the probe has reached Earth's orbit, it executes a series of controlled maneuvers, adjusting its attitude to manage the descent profile. It aims for a specific entry corridor to hit the designated splashdown zone. Once atmospheric drag has slowed the craft down significantly, parachutes are deployed to further shed velocity. Near the surface of the water, the probe ignites its retro rockets, ensuring a gentle splashdown off the Florida coast.
